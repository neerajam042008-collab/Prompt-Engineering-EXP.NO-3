PRECEDING QUESTION PROMPTING
QUESTION:
"Why is climate change a global concern? Explain how greenhouse gases contribute to global warming."

Climate change is a global concern because it affects the entire planet and has wide-ranging impacts on natural systems, human societies, and economies. Changes in climate patterns influence temperature, rainfall, ocean levels, and weather events. Since the Earth’s atmosphere and oceans are interconnected, environmental changes in one region can influence conditions in other parts of the world. For example, rising global temperatures can lead to melting glaciers and polar ice, which contributes to sea-level rise and threatens coastal communities worldwide. Climate change can also disrupt ecosystems, reduce biodiversity, affect food production, and increase the frequency of extreme weather events such as heatwaves, floods, droughts, and storms. These effects make climate change a shared global challenge that requires cooperation among countries to reduce risks and protect future generations. 🌍
A major cause of climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. Greenhouse gases include Carbon Dioxide (CO₂), Methane (CH₄), Nitrous Oxide (N₂O), and Water Vapor. These gases naturally exist in the atmosphere and help maintain the Earth’s temperature through a process known as the greenhouse effect. When sunlight reaches the Earth, some of the energy is absorbed by the land and oceans, warming the planet, while the rest is reflected back into space. The Earth then releases some of this energy as heat (infrared radiation). Greenhouse gases absorb part of this outgoing heat and re-emit it in different directions, including back toward the Earth’s surface. This process keeps the planet warm enough to support life.
However, human activities have significantly increased the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. Burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas for electricity, transportation, and industry releases large amounts of Carbon Dioxide. Agricultural activities and livestock farming produce Methane, while the use of fertilizers releases Nitrous Oxide. Deforestation also contributes to the problem because trees that normally absorb carbon dioxide are removed, allowing more CO₂ to remain in the atmosphere.
As the levels of these gases increase, more heat becomes trapped in the atmosphere, causing the Earth’s average temperature to rise. This rise in temperature is known as global warming, which leads to long-term changes in climate patterns. Over time, global warming can intensify weather events, alter rainfall patterns, affect water availability, and threaten wildlife habitats. Because these impacts affect ecosystems, economies, and human well-being across the globe, climate change is considered one of the most important environmental challenges facing humanity today.

RESULT: 
The experiment was conducted to study different prompt engineering techniques such as Straightforward Prompting, Tabular Format Prompting, Missing Word Prompting, and Preceding Question Prompting using ChatGPT.
From the observations, Straightforward Prompting produced clear and direct responses because the instructions were simple and easy for the AI to understand. Tabular Format Prompting helped present information in a well-structured format, which made comparisons easier and improved the readability of the output. Missing Word Prompting was effective for testing factual knowledge, as ChatGPT was able to identify the correct term based on the context of the sentence. Preceding Question Prompting helped guide the AI step by step, which improved the relevance and logical flow of the final response.
Overall, the results show that using appropriate prompting techniques with ChatGPT improves the clarity, structure, and accuracy of the generated responses. Each prompting method is useful for different tasks such as explanation, comparison, knowledge testing, and guided reasoning.
